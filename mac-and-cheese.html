Mac and Cheese

I had just set my goals for the new year, vowing that I would be fit, and be sexier than ever, when I thought, "hmm... I want some mac and cheese." Arteries be damned. Ironically, my heart had never felt so light and free after I had 3 servings of this delicious macaroni and cheese. This is what Emelia Eirhardt must have felt like after she flew for the first time. Like Emelia, this mac and cheese is a big deal-ia.

Thinking of leaving your "comfort zone?" Don't. Sit back at home, and enjoy this creamy, crunchy velvetty macaroni and cheese. Trust me. I'm a scientist.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of triple cheddar shredded cheese.
  • 3/4 cup butter
  • 6 tbsp flour
  • 5 cups whole milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp mustard powder
  • 1 lb elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 panko
  • Parsley, chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 400Â°F. Place the rack in the middle of the oven.
  2. Cook pasta 1 minute prior to al dente, drain and set aside.
  3. Melt 1/2 cup of butter over medium heat in a saucepan. Add flour and stir until thick.
  4. Slowly whisk in milk little by little, especially if the milk cold. The slower you add the milk, the quicker the sauce with thicken.
  5. Stir in the cheese, and add salt, pepper, garlic powder, mustard powder to the sauce, stir until combined, and the cheese is melted and the sauce is thickened.
  6. Add the cooked macaroni and stir them together.
  7. Spray a 9x13 baking dish with cooking spray. Then transer macaroni to baking dish.
  8. Melt the remaining butter and fold in the panko. Spread the mixture atop the macaroni.
  9. Bake in oven until golden brown.
  10. Add a pinch of parsley if desired.
